My Favorite Cars

A collection of some of the most iconic vehicles.

Toyota Sprinter Trueno AE86

The Toyota Sprinter Trueno AE86 is a classic Japanese lightweight sports car. It's known for its wonky, yet iconic, design and its fantastic handling. This car gained legendary status from its prominence in the anime series "Initial D," where it was the hero car used for mountain pass racing.

Datsun 240Z

The Datsun 240Z, also known as the Fairlady Z in Japan, is a classic two-seater sports car. It's celebrated for its sleek and timeless design, often considered one of the best-looking cars of its era. With its powerful straight-six engine and rear-wheel drive, it became an affordable and fun alternative to European sports cars.

Porsche 930 Turbo ("The Widowmaker")

The Porsche 930 Turbo earned the nickname "The Widowmaker" due to its powerful engine, short wheelbase, and a reputation for crazy oversteer. It was one of the first cars to be widely given this moniker. Its turbo lag and sudden power delivery made it a challenge for inexperienced drivers, leading to its legendary and slightly terrifying reputation.

Mazda RX-7 (FD)

The third-generation Mazda RX-7, chassis code FD, is revered for its stunning, aerodynamic design and exceptional performance. Its iconic design and appearance in movies like "The Fast and the Furious" cemented its place in pop culture. What makes it truly unique is its engine: the 13B-REW sequential twin-turbo Wankel rotary engine, which is known for its high-revving nature and distinct sound.
